Guess The Picture

This project revolves around a typical game where you have to guess what the picture is.

The implementation is aligned where there are 3 different game states: Start, Play, and End. Though the start and end game states are for the completeness and the consistency of a game, they help give info for what the player needs to do when they're starting or finishing the game.

How It Works: 

Players are required to use the Arduino implemented with 2 pushbuttons to switch from the starting screen to the game (as well as the end screen for whether they want to replay the game), and to confirm a guess that they have to type out with the keyboard, respectively. When the starting screen changes after pressing the respective pushbutton, the player is greeted with a user input and a randomized picture that they cannot see. The picture will fade in with a varied tint that changes over time to fully reveal the image over the allotted time of 60 seconds. If a guess is correct, a sound effect will play and an LED on the Arduino will have a green color, and the round will be over. If a guess is incorrect, a different sound effect will play and an LED on the Arduino will have a red color, so the player must keep guessing until the green LED lights up. Score is determined by the difference of the max amount of time for the round and the amount of seconds it takes for the player to guess correctly. If the player fails to guess what the image is in time, they do not get a score. The end screen will show up for the player to choose whether or not they want to restart the game.

Future Implementation: 

  • As the tinting of the image was not originally the method used for revealing images, there could be modes and selections where you can choose or randomize how a picture would be revealed, e.g, randomly revealing pixels of the image over time, or using covered splotches within the scope (area) of the image.

  • Rounds were meant to be implemented for a more insightful player experience, and can make the feel of the game elongated. There would be different kinds of background music playing as well.

  • Modes could be developed to make the player experience more rigorous and eventful if preferred. For example, with a time attack mode, for each consecutive round, the time could countdown faster, making the player instinctively guess while minimizing mistakes.
